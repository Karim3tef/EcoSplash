    Tips to help us to preserve Marine life.
    Respect Marine Life: Do not collect or remove any marine organisms, shells, or coral souvenirs. Leave the underwater environment as you found it. Dispose of Trash Properly: Dispose of your trash, including plastic waste, in designated bins on the shore. Participate in beach cleanups if they are organized in your area. Reduce Single-Use Plastics: Minimize your use of single-use plastics, such as plastic bottles and bags, to prevent marine pollution. Carry a reusable water bottle and shopping bag. Responsible boating and fishing: adhering to regulations and guidelines to prevent overfishing, safeguarding marine ecosystems, and ensuring sustainable practices for the long-term health of aquatic environments. Using non-harmful fishing methods: like sustainable gear and avoiding harmful practices such as using poisons or bombs, helps protect marine ecosystems and maintain fish populations for future generations. Remember that your actions, even as an individual swimmer, can have a positive impact on the preservation of marine species and ecosystems. By adopting responsible and sustainable practices, you can contribute to the long-term health and conservation of the Red Sea and other precious marine environments.
